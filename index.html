About Our Project

Every year, millions of people suffer strokes in areas without access to advanced imaging or neurologists. In many low-resource hospitals across Sub-Saharan Africa, diagnosing the type of stroke—ischemic or hemorrhagic—is often delayed or impossible, limiting treatment and outcomes.
The Stroke Equity Project bridges that gap using an AI model trained on clinical features from over 19,000 patients in the International Stroke Trial, one of the largest stroke datasets in the world. By analyzing simple bedside information—like patient age, blood pressure, heart rate, time since onset, and consciousness level—the model predicts stroke subtype with clinically meaningful accuracy, without the need for CT scans.
But this is not just a technology project. It's a collaboration. We aim to work with hospitals, clinicians, and public health partners in Sub-Saharan Africa to adapt, evaluate, and deploy the tool where it's needed most.
